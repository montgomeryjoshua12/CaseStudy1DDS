Repository for DDS 6306 Case Study 1

This project analyzes employee attrition for Frito-Lay using exploratory data analysis and classification modeling in R. The goal was to identify key drivers of attrition and build a model capable of predicting which employees are at higher risk of leaving.

The analysis identified the strongest attrition-related factors as Overtime, Job Level, and Stock Option Level, with Work-Life Balance and Job Involvement also showing strong relationships.

Two models were evaluated: Naive Bayes and KNN. The final Naive Bayes model outperformed KNN, achieving:
- Accuracy: 72%
- Sensitivity: 78%
- Specificity: 71%

Because both sensitivity and specificity exceeded 60%, the selected model met the client requirement.

Additional findings suggest that employees working overtime, in lower job levels, and with fewer stock options were more likely to leave. These insights can help Frito-Lay proactively target retention efforts and reduce turnover-related costs.
